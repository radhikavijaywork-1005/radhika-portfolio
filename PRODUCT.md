# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Primarily hiring managers and recruiters evaluating Radhika for senior product
design roles; secondarily design peers and industry contacts who might
reference or share her work. They skim quickly, comparing her against other
candidates, and are looking for evidence of real shipped impact and genuine
design judgment — not just visual polish.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that gets Radhika hired. It exists to prove 7+ years of
product design work (STAGE, Adani/Trainman) through real case studies with
measurable outcomes, and to establish her as an AI-builder — someone who
ships, not just designs in Figma. Success is a hiring manager remembering her
and reaching out.

## Positioning

A senior product designer who ships measurable outcomes and builds her own
tools with AI — not a Figma-only designer, and not just an engineer who can
design.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: View Work (case studies); Resume/Email as the
  fallback for a recruiter ready to act immediately.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: this person has real,
  measurable design impact, and she's genuinely interesting.
- Belief ladder: she has real shipped work with real metrics (28% opt-in
  lift, 57% failure reduction) → she can build/ship with AI, not just design
  → she has genuine personal taste, not a templated portfolio → reach out.
- Proof on hand: real case studies already in `src/data/content.js`
  (Trainman/Adani projects with metrics, The Design Bridge project). Photo
  and case-study screenshots are honest placeholders until real assets are
  supplied.

## Brand Personality

Playful, grounded, sharp. Warm and a little fun — nature-toned palette,
hand-touches, genuine interactive moments — backed by real technical and
design rigor, not decoration for its own sake. Her Indian heritage signals
(the Devanagari greeting, the Sanskrit quote) are personal, not a "theme" —
they should stay central rather than be treated as an ornamental skin.

## Anti-references

The most recent prior version (orange/black, high-contrast editorial-minimal,
huge outlined typography) — not because it was poorly executed, but because
it felt impersonal and generic, disconnected from who she actually is.
Avoid: cold/aggressive high-contrast palettes chosen for trend rather than
truth, pills used as a repeated system, uniform card grids, fake mockup
screenshots pretending to be real product screens.

## Design Principles

Personal before trendy — every visual choice should trace back to something
true about her (heritage, AI-builder identity, real project outcomes), not
just what's currently fashionable in portfolio design.

Real proof outweighs polish — measurable outcomes (28%, 57%, etc.) carry real
visual weight; decoration never outshines evidence.

Playful in motion, serious in substance — interactivity and delight live in
the details (hover, tilt, micro-motion) without undermining the credibility
of the case-study content itself.

Show, don't tell — express "AI-builder" through how the site behaves
(thoughtful interactivity, technical craft) rather than only claiming it in
copy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: body text at minimum 4.5:1 contrast, visible focus states,
`prefers-reduced-motion` respected for any tilt/parallax/animation effects.
