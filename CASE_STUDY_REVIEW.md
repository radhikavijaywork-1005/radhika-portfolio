# STAGE Paywall Case Study — Hiring-Manager Review

Two independent reviews of `/work/paywall-experiments`, done as if evaluating this case study for a Senior Product Designer hire. To revisit and fix later.

## Priority fix list (cheapest → highest credibility payoff)

1. Add sample size / test duration next to each phase's stat card (Phase 1/2/3 Impact cards)
2. Either show Variant 3–5 numbers in Phase 1, or soften the "backfired" claim in Challenges & Learnings to only what's actually shown
3. Drop the "250%" relative-increase stat in Overall Impact — reframe as straight 8%→28%
4. Add a real contact/hire-me CTA after the case study ends (currently just links to next case study)
5. Turn the Timeline section into a simple visual chart instead of a plain text list
6. Add a methodology/rigor note generally — how "winner" was chosen, confidence level

## Review 1 — my own pass (head of design)

**Overall**: Strong mid-to-senior case study. The 3-phase experimentation narrative, real vernacular research quotes, and the honest "we reverted from urgency/social-proof because it backfired" moment are genuinely good signals. Reservations below.

1. **No statistical backbone.** Every stat (17.8%, 21.1%, 28.6%, "40% fewer cancellations") is stated as fact with zero sample size, test duration, or confidence signal.
2. **Ownership is fuzzy.** "Sole product designer" + "2 PM · Data · 2 Eng" stated once at the top, never revisited per-phase. Unclear which calls were hers vs. the PMs'.
3. **No final "here's what shipped" moment.** You see variants being tested and problems diagnosed, but never land on a clean "here's the actual paywall live today, start to finish."
4. **No path to contact.** Case study ends on "Next case study" — no closing "want to talk about this? here's how to reach me."
5. **Accessibility/Tier-2-3 literacy angle is absent**, despite her own research surfacing trust/comprehension issues in a lower-literacy audience — exactly the kind of inclusive-design thinking that differentiates a senior candidate.

**Single biggest fix**: Add a line of methodology rigor (sample size + test duration) near the impact stats — cheapest fix, highest credibility payoff.

## Review 2 — second opinion (Lead Product Designer persona, subagent)

**Verdict: Advance to next round, with reservations.** Well-structured, above-average case study — better than most mid-level portfolios. The Challenges & Learnings section shows real strategic maturity. But the metrics don't hold up to five minutes of skeptical questioning.

1. **Narrative arc**: Shape is right (Problem → Research → 3 Decisions → Impact → Timeline → Reflection) with a genuinely skimmable Summary block up top. But the page is very long (30+ screen-heights), and momentum sags in "Design work," where six numbered critique points read like an audit checklist rather than a story. Decisions is the strongest writing and should be reached faster.
2. **Evidence of seniority**: Mixed. "MY ROLE" framing is good, and Challenges & Learnings (unlearning growth defaults, protecting the main funnel by testing Special Access only on the 3rd paywall view) is the best evidence of strategic thinking. Missing: a real stakeholder-friction moment — no visible instance of pushing back on a PM or resolving disagreement with data. Reads as well-run team effort more than designer driving the room.
3. **Credibility of metrics — the weak point.** No sample size, no test duration per phase, no confidence level, no explanation of how "winner" was determined beyond eyeballing deltas. The "250%" stat in Overall Impact is a *relative*-increase reframe of an 8%→28% (20-point) *absolute* move — a marketing trick, not an analyst's number, and a senior interviewer will flag it immediately. "14L revenue uplift" is labeled "Projected" but presented visually identical to the realized stats, blurring projected vs. actual.
4. **Failure and learning depth**: Challenges section claims urgency/anchoring/social-proof "backfired and pulled conversion down" — but Phase 1's own Impact card only shows results for Variants 1 and 2. Variants 3–5 (the ones implied to have failed) never get their numbers shown anywhere. The failure is asserted, not evidenced.
5. **Craft/visual execution gaps**: Overall aesthetic is clean and consistent. Two concrete gaps: (a) the five Phase 1 variant phone mockups are visually near-identical — same actor, same layout, differing mainly in banner copy — making "5 variants" feel thinner as a design artifact than as a headline claim; (b) the Timeline section (the emotional payoff, 8%→28.6%) is a plain text list with horizontal rules instead of a chart — a missed opportunity to make the growth curve land visually.
6. **What's missing entirely**: No contact/hire-me CTA after the case study closes (footer only has social links + next case study). No explicit "my contribution vs. team's" breakdown inside the narrative itself (only in the top meta block). No accessibility or localization consideration despite this being a multi-dialect, multi-script product. No before/after visual pairing of original vs. final paywall side by side.
7. **Single biggest fix**: Add a visible methodology strip to each phase (sample size / test duration / how "winner" was chosen) directly next to the stat cards. The case study's credibility rests entirely on 8%→28%, and right now that number is asserted, not defended.
